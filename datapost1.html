Budget allocation report

Consolidating spending and budget information

November 1, 2024 Power BI

An operations team needed a more consistent way to track licensing costs for technical resources (Microsoft enterprise licenses, Adobe, etc.). They had several csv files listing users, the resources, and the costs. Their requirements were to consolidate the spending reports, balance them against the allocated budgets for each of the teams, and see when overspending was occurring.

Merging the reports

The team had a collection of csv files all formatted the same, which made things a little easier. I pulled the folder into Power Query and appended the reports into a singular table.

Modeling

Since the data was fairly good sized, and it could potentially grow more over time, I went ahead and set up a model. I pulled the the cost information into the fact table, then made dimension tables for the teams, products, and managers. The budget table I made manually since it was just a matter of entering in the single budget for each team. The date table I made using calendarauto().

Relationships

I designated a unique identifier for each dimension with indexing: teamID, managerID, productID. I connected each of them to the fact table using their ID values. I also connected the order date to the date table and the budget table to each corresponding team value.

Report

Once all the modeling was done, I threw on a KPI card with the amount spent. The established budget was added as the target value, with a delta value showing how far under or over the spending was from the budget value. The team also wanted a way to see which managers had ordered which units, so I added a table at the bottom to aggregate by manager and team. Users can search for a particular manager or team within the table. Users can also filter by team using the main filter at the top of the page.

Just the two visuals met the requirements for this part of the project, but the team anticipates needing more complex reporting in the future for their contracts with vendors and general program KPIs. I added a navigation for the forthcoming report requirements so all their team information can be kept in one place.
